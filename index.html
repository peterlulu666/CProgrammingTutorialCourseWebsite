Course Description

This course is an introduction to computing and programming. One goal is to introduce you to the basic concepts of computation and the principles of problem solving through programming. The other goal is to ensure that you have the necessary knowlege and skills that are required by subsequent ALIGN courses.

The course does not assume prior programming experience. All of the concepts and techniques will be presented in class, supplemented by assigned readings, and through hands-on programming assignments. For those with a little prior programming experience, some of this material will be a reivew, but this course will give you a more systematic view of programming, and provide an opportunity to hone your skills.

You will also learn to use professional tools for developing, debugging, documenting, testing, and archiving the programs you create. These tools are the same ones that you will use throughout your academic program, and that employers expect you to know how to use. Learning them well is a good investment in your career.

Prerequisites

The course assumes a basic facility with arithmetic, algebra, and a logical approach to problem solving. The course concentrates a lot of leaning into a very short time period. It requires curiosity, self-discipline, and persistence on your part to master the material.

It also requires some amount of people skills because all computer scientists end up working with others. You will benefit by finding others to study with and discuss ideas and concepts presented in this course. Although you are required to do your own work on programming assignments and exams, it is fair to ask others for help spotting a problem in code that is not working correctly, and to offer the same help in return.

Other Requirements

You will need a fairly recent model portable computer with sufficient memory (8GB-16GB) and wireless network access. You will do your work on this computer, and bring it to all class sessons and exams. The development tools run on MS Windows 7/8/10, MacOS 10.12, and most Linux distributions. You will also need a basic facility in the use of the computer, including its basic tools, and windowing system.
